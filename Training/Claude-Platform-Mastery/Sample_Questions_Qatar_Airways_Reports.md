# Sample Questions — Qatar Airways Comparative Reports

Questions you can ask about the two deliverables: the **Word report**
(`Qatar_Airways_vs_Industry.docx`) and the **Excel workbook**
(`Qatar_Airways_Revenue_Comparison.xlsx`). Grouped so you know which file
each question draws on.

---

## Word Report — Qatar Airways vs. the Global Aviation Industry

### Numbers & comparisons
- What's Qatar Airways' profit margin vs Emirates', and why is Emirates' so much higher despite lower revenue?
- Rank all four carriers by revenue per passenger — what does that tell you about business mix?
- Emirates has bigger revenue but Qatar has more awards — build me a case for which is the "better" airline depending on what an investor vs a flyer cares about.

### Strategy / "why" questions
- Qatar's profit dropped from FY24/25 to FY25/26 while passengers grew — what does that combination usually signal?
- Given Etihad's safety ranking vs Qatar's product ranking, where should Qatar Airways be investing next?
- Qatar Duty Free won "Best Airport Shopping" three years running — how much could that realistically be worth to Group revenue, and is it captured in these numbers?

### Gaps and assumptions (stress-testing the data)
- Where did you have to write "n/d" instead of a real number, and how would the picture change if we had that data?
- Singapore Airlines has no cargo/passenger split here — find that and add it in.
- You used Emirates "airline only" revenue but Qatar "Group" revenue — how big is that mismatch, and does it change the ranking?

### Decision-style questions
- If I were pitching Qatar Airways to a travel client over Emirates, what are my three strongest data points from this report?
- What's the weakest argument in the report — the one a skeptic would attack first?

## Excel Workbook — Revenue Comparison

### Revenue Overview tab
- Which airline has the best profit margin, and is that because of lower costs or a smaller, more premium network?
- What's the combined revenue of all four carriers, and what % of that does Qatar Airways represent?
- Emirates carries way more passengers than Etihad but revenue-per-passenger is different — walk me through why.
- If Qatar Airways grew revenue at Etihad's 25% rate next year, what would its new total be?

### Revenue by Segment tab
- Cargo is 9% of Qatar's revenue vs 13% for Emirates — in dollar terms, how much would Qatar need to add to cargo to match Emirates' percentage?
- Etihad's cargo grew 24% but Qatar's grew 17% — which airline is cargo becoming more important for, relatively speaking?
- Singapore Airlines has no segment split in this sheet — estimate a plausible passenger/cargo split based on the other three carriers and flag it as an estimate.

### Growth & Rankings tab
- Which airline has the best growth-to-profitability ratio — fast growth AND healthy margins, not just one or the other?
- Etihad's profit tripled but it's still the smallest carrier here — is that a story of recovery or genuine competitive threat to Qatar?
- Cross-reference this tab with Revenue Overview: does the airline with the best Skytrax ranking also have the best financials, or are those two things decoupled?

### Formula / model-testing questions
- Change Qatar's net profit to $3B in the sheet and tell me what happens to every other number that depends on it.
- Which cells are formulas and which are hardcoded — show me the difference in the workbook.
- Add a new row for Turkish Airlines using public data, and check that all the formulas extend correctly.

---

*Tip: the common thread across all of these is that each one has a specific
number, a comparison, or a decision attached — that's what produces a sharp
answer instead of a generic summary.*
