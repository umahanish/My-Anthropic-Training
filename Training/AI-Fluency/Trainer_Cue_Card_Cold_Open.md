# Trainer Cue Card — Session 1 Cold Open
*Glance-and-go. Print this one page, keep it face-up on the podium.*

## BEFORE YOU CLICK ANYTHING

Lights up, room settling, **no slide showing yet** (or a blank/black screen). Don't open with the title slide. Just start talking.

---

## THE COLD OPEN — say this close to word-for-word

> "Picture this. It's 4:47pm on a Friday.
>
> A developer on your team gets an error in production. He pastes the stack trace into a chatbot. It gives him a fix that sounds right. He pastes it in, runs the tests he always runs, they pass, he merges, he goes home.
>
> Monday morning: that fix introduced a silent data leak. Nobody knew AI was involved — because nobody said anything. It "worked," right up until it didn't.
>
> *(pause — let it sit for a second)*
>
> Here's the thing. Every single person in this room already has access to AI that's more capable than that developer's chatbot. That was never the bottleneck.
>
> What you're about to spend today learning is the thing that *was* the bottleneck. And it's a skill — not a talent. You don't need to already be 'good at AI' to walk out of this room fluent in it."

**→ Now click to the title slide.** Let "AI Fluency" land as the answer to what they just felt, not a topic on an agenda.

---

## THE BEGINNER PITCH — say this right after, before the agenda slide

> "This isn't about becoming an AI expert. Everyone here already has AI access — the gap was never access, it's judgment. That's learnable in a day. And it compounds: the more fluently you use Claude specifically, the more it's a force-multiplier instead of a liability generator with a coin-flip chance of being right."

---

## DAY-LONG DELIVERY REMINDERS *(quick glance, not scripted)*

| When | Do this |
|---|---|
| Every module | Call the nightmare developer **"Alex"** by name — keep referencing him. Callbacks feel like a plot. |
| Before any AI demo | Ask the room to guess first — "What do you think happens if I ask Claude X?" Hands up, *then* run it live. |
| At least once | Run one prompt live that you have **not** pre-tested. Real suspense, real learning either way. |
| End of every module | Tease the next one — don't summarize. *"Next, we're going to deliberately break something — and you won't know what's wrong until you find it."* |
| Every ~10-12 min | Switch mode: talk → show → they do → discuss. Energy drops if lecture runs long. |
| Right after lunch (Module 4) | Skip the Automation Bias slide as the opener. Hand them a snippet cold — "90 seconds, find what's wrong" — *before* explaining the checklist. |
| End of day | Ask for **one personal commitment**, not a framework recap. That's what they'll repeat to a colleague — and what fills your next session. |
